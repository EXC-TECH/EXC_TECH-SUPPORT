Calibrating Your MakerBot Digitizer

    Calibrating your MakerBot Digitizer ensures that you will get the optimal performance. 

    The first time you connect your Digitizer to MakerWare for Digitizer you will be automatically prompted to run the calibration script, you will need to do this to get started. 

    If you are experiencing scan quality issues it may also be helpful to run the Calibrate Camera or Calibrate Turntable/Lasers routines. These can be found under the scanner menu in MakerWare for Digitizer.

    For the best performance, you should run both calibration routines every time you move your Digitizer to a new location. It is also recommended that you run the Calibrate Turntable/Lasers routine each time once a week or after every 20 scans, or if you Digitizer is bumped or dropped. 

    Before Calibration

    Location

    • Make sure your Digitizer is on a flat stable work surface
    • Have it face the nearest wall, but make sure that it is not too close to the wall.If the Digitizer lasers appear on the wall, the camera may pick them up and interpret them as part of you scan
