Setup, First Run Experience

    Guided Setup Video

    Getting Started

    Once you have updated to the most recent firmware on your MakerBot Sketch, the printer will walk you through the First Run Experience. 

    Use the Touchscreen to select "Setup" to go through the First Run Experience or select "Skip" to skip it. 

    It is recommended that you go through the First Run Experience as this will walk you through the basic printer functions and ensure your MakerBot Sketch is fully set up. 

    If you do hit "Skip" or want to go back through the experience for any reason you can initiate it again by resetting your MakerBot Sketch to factory settings. This option can be found under "Settings" --> "Utilities" --> "Reset to Factory Settings". "Reset to Factory Settings" can be found on the 3rd page of the "Utilities" menu. 

    Wi-Fi Setup

    If you updated your firmware via Wi-Fi during the initial setup this step will automatically be skipped and you will be presented with the Account Setup step. 

    If you updated firmware initially via Ethernet or USB Stick you will now be prompted to connect your MakerBot Sketch to a Wi-Fi network. 

    Select "Setup Wi-Fi" to connect to Wi-Fi or "Skip" to proceed to the Account Setup step. You can always add a Wi-Fi network to your MakerBot Sketch another time by navigating to Settings --> Network --> Wi-Fi. 

    Next, select your Wi-Fi network from a list of nearby networks. 

    Click the network you would like to connect to to proceed. 

    On the next screen, enter the password for your Wi-Fi network and then press the check mark with the circle to enter it and proceed. 

    If you receive a "This Connection Failed" message, the most likely cause is an incorrect Wi-Fi password. Select your network again and re-enter the password. 

    If you are sure the password has been entered correctly and you are still receiving this message, proceed with the FRE and try setting up the Wi-Fi again by navigating to Settings --> Network --> Wi-Fi on the Touch Screen. If you can not connect from there, click here to open a case with our Support Team and someone will reach out you as soon as possible. 

    Connect to MakerBot Account

    Once you have connected your MakerBot Sketch to a Wi-Fi network or if it is connected via an Ethernet cable you can enter your MakerBot account credentials to link your MakerBot Sketch to your account. 

    If your printer is not connected or you would not like to link your account you can select "Skip" to proceed to the next step. 

    You can link your account at a later time if you like by going to Settings --> Network --> MakerBot Account. 

    To connect your MakerBot Account, enter your email address or username and you password. Then select "Log In". If you do not already have a MakerBot Account, you can create one here. 

    Give your printer a name

    Once you have connected your MakerBot Sketch to your MakerBot Account, you will be presented with the option to assign a unique name to your Printer. This is helpful to differentiate different printers when sending prints. 

    Type your desired printer name into the  "Printer Name" field to name your MakerBot Sketch.  Then select "Next" to proceed. 

    You can also select "Skip" to skip this step. 

    You can change the printer name at any point by going to Settings --> Utilities --> Printer Name. 

    Calibration

    To ensure that the distance between the nozzle and build surface is correct, your MakerBot Sketch will now run the Calibration routine. This will automatically start after the printer naming step. You can also run the Calibration routine at any point by navigating to "Settings" --> "Utilities" --> "Calibration" on the touchscreen. 

    It is not recommended to skip this step as an improper calibration distance can cause significant print issues such as material failing to extrude or the model becoming detached from the build surface. 

    The extruder will move to the side before returning to the middle and lowering it to the build surface. This is the homing process where the extruder determines its position in the build area. 

    Insert the Quick Start Guide that came with your printer between the nozzle and build surface. If you do not have the Quick Start Guide available, you can use a piece of paper with a similar thickness (.25mm). 

    You are looking for the nozzle to be putting the same amount of pressure on the the paper as a ball point pen would apply to a piece of paper when writing. You should be able to feel some pressure from the nozzle, but you can still move the paper. 

    If the nozzle is too close (can not move the paper) use the up arrow to raise it. If the nozzle is too far (can not feel the paper on the nozzle) use the down arrow to lower it. 


    Once the nozzle is at the right height, hit "Next" to proceed. 

    Loading Material

    The next step in setting up your MakerBot Sketch is to load the Material. To start this process, the printer will begin be heating the extruder. 

    If you would like to load material at a different time, you can select "Skip". This will end the First Run Experience as the final step is running a test print which will require material to be loaded. 

    While the extruder is heating:

    • Remove the Spool Enclosure Cover

    • Unwrap the Material Spool that came in the accessory kit or another PLA spool (the test print in the next step is sliced for PLA so another material can not be used at this phase)
    • Place the spool into place around the Material Hub
      • Ensure that the loose end of the material will feed upwards on the left side of the Spool Enclosure

    Once the extruder has finished heating the screen will shift. 

    Insert the free end of the Material Spool into the hole in the top left corner. 

    Keep inserting the material until you feel it being pulled by the feed motor. 

    The screen will automatically shift or you can select "Next". 

    Wait for material to start extruding from the nozzle and then select "Next". This can take some time.  Remove any extruded material from the nozzle. 

    Place the Spool Enclosure Cover back into place. 

    Test Print

    The final step of the first run experience is to initiate a test print. 

    Select "Print Now" to begin the test print or "Skip" to end the First Run Experience. 

    If you choose "Skip" you can begin printing your own models through MakerBot Print or MakerBot Cloud. 

    If you choose "Print Now" the test print will begin printing.

    If you experience any issues with the Test Print, please click here to review our troubleshooting articles. 

    Once the Test Print has successfully completed, the following screen will be displayed. 

    Click "Finish" to end the First Run Experience. 

    You are now all set to start using your MakerBot Sketch 3D Printer!

    If you need additional assistance you can view our basic usage articles here or open a case with our Support Team here.
